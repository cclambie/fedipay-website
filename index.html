How It Works

FediPay has three simple parts working together:

1

Track (Privately)

Install the FediPay browser extension or use a compatible app. It records lightweight "usage events"‚Äîlike reading a post or watching a video‚Äîwithout uploading the content itself.

Your data stays on your device or your personal server. No central tracking, no surveillance.

2

Allocate (Fairly)

Set a monthly budget (e.g., ¬£10). At the end of the month, FediPay calculates who you engaged with and suggests a fair percentage for each creator.

You have full control: set caps per creator, exclude accounts, pin favorites, or manually override the suggestions.

3

Reward (Your Choice)

Review your "payout plan" and approve it. In the current phase, FediPay helps you decide who to pay and how much‚Äîyou handle the actual payment via existing methods.

Automated federated settlement (server-to-server transfers) is on the roadmap.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is FediPay a payment platform?

Not at first. The initial goal is to make it easy to compute a fair allocation plan‚Äîwithout requiring custody of funds. Think of it as "Spotify Wrapped for patronage" that helps you decide who to support.

Does FediPay track everything I do?

No. Tracking is opt-in, designed to be minimal, and intended to stay under your control. You choose what to enable, what to exclude, and where your data lives.

Can I use it for things outside the Fediverse?

Not yet, but it's on the roadmap. We're starting with ActivityPub (Mastodon, PeerTube, etc.), then expanding to RSS/WordPress, podcasts, and FOSS projects.

How is this different from Patreon?

Patreon is a centralized platform that takes a cut and controls the relationship. FediPay is a protocol‚Äîyou own your data, choose your server, and creators don't need to "join" anything.

What about transaction fees?

Transaction fees are unavoidable (banks, payment processors, etc.). FediPay is designed to minimize them through batching, thresholds, and (eventually) federated settlement between servers.

Can I help build this?

Absolutely! FediPay is FOSS and built in public. Check out the GitHub repos, read the docs, and get in touch.
